I am a lecturer (Assistant Professor) in Bioelectronics at Dept. of Electrical & Electronic Engineering, University College London. I received a PhD from Ecole Polytechnique Federale de Lausanne (EPFL) in 2015 and have been with the Dept. of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Imperial College as a Imperial College Research Fellow during 2015-2018. I received my BSc and MSc degrees (both with distinction) in Electrical Engineering and Microelectronics from Sharif University of Technology in 2007 and 2009, respectively.

I am a Fellow of the Higher Education Academy (FHEA) and a member of IEEE, and IEEE CAS, SSC and EMB societies. I have been on the technical programme committees of several IEEE conferences including BioCAS 2021, ICECS 2019-2020, and a member of the IEEE BioCASS technical committee. I have been elected to the IEEE CAS Board of Governors (BoG) for the period of 2020-2022.



Research

I lead an interdiciplinary reserach team, Biosensors and Instrumentation, with a focus on analogue/mixed-signal Integrated Ciruit (IC) design and the CMOS biosensors. We apply the fundamental tools and techniques in creating new wearable technologies for real-time quantitative analysis of health conditions such as physiological stress, oral health and acute pain. In line with this goal, we also reserach biomarkers of such health conditions and diseases in non-blood biofluids, in particular saliva. Currently, the main research themes in my team include:

  • Low-power microelectronics design
  • Monolithic integration of biosensors and (micro-)electronics
  • Feasibility of new wearable technologies for real-time analysis of biolfuids
  • Clinical study of salivary biomarkers of acute pain
