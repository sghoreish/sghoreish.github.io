I am a lecturer (Assistant Professor) in Wearable Technologies at University College London. I received a PhD from Ecole Polytechnique Federale de Lausanne (EPFL) in 2015 and have been with the Dept. of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Imperial College as a Junior Research Fellow during Apr 2015 to Oct 2018. I received my BSc and MSc degrees (both with distinction) in Electrical Engineering and Microelectronics from Sharif University of Technology in 2007 and 2009, respectively.

I am a member of IET, IEEE, and IEEE CAS, SSC and EMB societies. I have been on the technical programme committee and review committee of IEEE BioCAS 2017-2018 and IEEE ICECS 2016-2018 conferences. I am an associate editor of the Journal of Microelectronics since 2015, and an elected member of IEEE BioCAS technical committee since 2018. I am a (co-)organiser of the first UKCAS workshop in 2018.



Research

My research interests include analogue/mixed-signal circuits design for wearable and implantable biosensors and the monolithic integration of biosensor with microelectronics, on which I have more than 30 peer-reviewed publications.

My current research themes include:

  • Wearable and implantable biosensors
  • Monilithic integration of biomolecule sensors and electronics
  • Low-power, low-noise electrical and electrochemical sensor instrumentation
  • Sensor and microelectronics co-design
