I am a lecturer in Bioelectronics at Dept. of Electrical & Electronic Engineering, University College London (UCL). I received a PhD from Ecole Polytechnique Federale de Lausanne (EPFL) in 2015 and have been with Imperial College as a Imperial College Research Fellow during 2015-2018 prior to joining UCL. I am a Fellow of the Higher Education Academy (FHEA) and a senior member of IEEE.

I am an active member of the IEEE Circuits and Systems (CAS) Society and an elected member of the Biomedical CAS (BioCAS) Technical Committee: I have been on the TPC of several IEEE conferences including Biosensors 2023, BioCAS 2021-2023, ICECS 2019-2022, and ISCAS. I am also an associate editor for the IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Circuits and Systems (TBioCAS), IEEE Open Journal of Circuits and Systems (OJCAS) and a guest editor of the IEEE Sensors Letters. I was elected to the IEEE CAS Board of Governors (BoG) for the period of 2020-2022.



Research

Currently, I lead an interdiciplinary reserach team on analogue/mixed-signal Integrated Ciruit (IC) design and CMOS biosensors. We apply the developed fundamental tools and techniques in creating new wearable technologies for real-time quantitative analysis of health and wellness, including conditions such as physiological stress, oral health and acute pain. In line with this goal, we also carry out both experimental and systematic review reserach on existing as well as new biomarkers of such health conditions and diseases in non-blood biofluids, such as saliva. I have provided scientific consultation to a number of healthcare industries in related fields. The main research themes in my team currently include:

  • Low-power microelectronics design
  • Monolithic integration of biosensors and (micro-)electronics on silicon
  • Development of new wearable technologies for real-time analysis of biolfuids
  • Various biofluid biomarkers related to nociception and sleep quality
